Information Systems Frontiers · Vol. 26, pp. 2205–2226 (2024) · Open Access

Dynamic Capabilities for the Twin Transformation Climb: A Capability Maturity Model

Katharina Breiter · Carlotta Crome · Anna Maria Oberländer · Feline Schnaak
DOI: 10.1007/s10796-024-10520-y
Open publication ↗ Also available via Springer (Open Access) ↗
▸ View abstract

Digital transformation and sustainability transformation are at the top of organizations' agendas to remain competitive. While guidance on both transformations exists separately, even more research on integrating digital and sustainability transformation, namely twin transformation, is required. Specifically, deeper knowledge about relevant twin transformation capabilities and progress is needed for effective implementation. To enhance the understanding and provide corresponding guidance, we developed a twin transformation capability maturity model focusing on dynamic capabilities required to realize twin transformation based on a structured literature review and interviews with 13 experts. Further, we demonstrated its use with a technology service provider. Our contribution is twofold: First, accounting for organizations' twin transformation starting points in terms of their digitalization and sustainability experience and expertise, we reveal three pathways to becoming a twin transformer. Second, our work provides an overview of 45 relevant twin transformation capabilities structured along six capability dimensions and four maturity stages. Our work also provides relevant practical implications supporting organizations in assessing their twin transformation maturity building the foundation for targeted capability development.

The model

One climb, four camps.

Twin transformation is the value-adding interplay of digital transformation and sustainability transformation — where digital technologies enable sustainability, and sustainability goals guide digital progress. The model tracks this across 45 capabilities, six dimensions, and four maturity stages.

STAGE 1
Awareness
Aware of the potential of combining digitalization and sustainability; first integrated pilots.
Details
Key shift: from separate initiatives to a first, deliberately integrated pilot.
→ Next: a shared roadmap
STAGE 2
Development
A twin transformation strategy and roadmap; long-term projects piloted and evaluated.
Details
Key shift: from isolated pilots to a strategy with measurable, shared objectives.
→ Next: organization-wide rollout
STAGE 3
Implementation
The strategy is operationalized and rolled out across the whole organization.
Details
Key shift: from a roadmap to organization-wide operational rollout.
→ Next: continuous self-optimization
STAGE 4
True twin transformer
Twin transformation criteria drive every decision, with continuous self-optimization.
Details
Key shift: from rollout to twin transformation as the default lens on every decision.
At the summit

Six capability dimensions

What you're scored on. Hover or focus a dimension to read what it covers — each keeps the same color throughout the assessment and your results.

Strategy & Leadership
A digital and sustainability strategy whose principles enable twin transformation — leadership involvement, cross-departmental and cross-organizational collaboration, and information sharing.
Culture & Employees
Collective values and beliefs, attitudes and behaviors — plus employees continually enhancing their operational and management skills and knowledge about twin transformation.
Ecosystem & Partnerships
A multilayer network of partners — grown historically or nurtured by management choices — to leverage twin transformation market opportunities.
Products & Services
A new generation of digital and sustainable products or services characterized by servitization — digital services combined with sustainability endeavors.
Operations
Value-creation processes, systems thinking and design techniques that enable digital, sustainable value creation and decision-making across the whole organization.
Technology
Methods and information systems that support the design, execution, monitoring and improvement of digitalization and sustainability activities.

Three pathways to becoming a twin transformer

Organizations start from different points depending on their prior digital and sustainability experience — all three routes converge at Stage 2, then share one climb to the summit.

Newcomer
Twin transformation newcomer
Limited digital and sustainability capabilities — integrate both from the start to save time and resources.
Typical pattern: few existing routines to unlearn, but capabilities in both areas need building in parallel.
DT expert
Digital transformation expert
A strong digital foundation — make digital transformation sustainable by closing the sustainability gap.
Typical pattern: mature digital infrastructure and skills; sustainability criteria are the missing layer.
ST expert
Sustainability transformation expert
A strong sustainability foundation — make sustainability transformation digital by closing the digitalization gap.
Typical pattern: established sustainability governance; digital tooling to scale and monitor it is the gap.
How this becomes actionable: your result shows which of the three routes you're on and identifies your bottleneck dimension — the one capability area holding back your overall stage, and the clearest place to focus next.
